Case study for VOXAPP
- a language learning app -

Overview

VOXAPP, a language learning app created in four weeks, makes learning vocabulary easy and fun. By implementing authentic, responsive audio elements and fun, gaming-like features the user can easily build the knowledge he really needs for feeling confident in the everyday communication with collegues, friends and family. For collecting and memorizing the words the user really needs, recording them via voice is possible. This is also very useful to practice the correct pronounciation!

RESEARCH

As one of the first steps I took a look at two competitors apps: Actionbound and c:geo. Especially Actionbound has a great educational concept which gave me valuable insights in how to trigger the user with rewards. By evaluating UX-related issues, I could define an appropriate standard.

IDEATION

Example of one of the task analysis/user flows: based on user interviews, they were defined to determine the most important actions users would take to complete their goals using the app. Different options in choosing the perfect learning style from found their way into the information architecture.

PROTOTYPE

By using the method of rapid prototyping, I created low-fidelity paper wireframes to lay out the key functions of the app. The paper screens were then turned into an simple, clickable prototype with Prott. During a moderated remote and in-person test, the prototype was tested by three  participants. There were several issues which came up during testing. They were ranked using Jakob Nielsen's severity scale.

WHAT'S NEXT?

After implementing the changes in the prototype, there will be a next round of testing. When moving forward from low fidelity wireframes to mid and then to high fidelity, more functions and features will find their way into the prototype. 

LEARNINGS

As this was my first UX project, I learned to examine closely common UI elements and putting them into use in my work. Also I became aware of the importance of structuring the content properly regarding to best practices in the information architecture. To acknowledge the importance of user testing and receiving valuable feedback was probably the biggest eye opener for me as it proves that all work should occur to create something meaningful for others.

ROLE

This was a 4-week project, evolving from scratch. I was the designer in response for research, prototyping and testing.
